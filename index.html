In Brief:

  • A devastating landslide hit Taliye village in Raigad district, Maharashtra on July 22, 2021
  • The landslide was triggered by incessant rainfall over the Western Ghats.
  • Over 84 people lost their lives.
  • Dozens of houses were completely buried under debris.

Taliye village is located in Raigad District,Maharashtra. At an elevation of 310 meters above mean sea level, approximately 150 meters from the back slope of the hill. A landslide occurred at Taliye village on July 22, 2021, at 8:30 PM due to heavy and continuous rainfall, which led to water infiltration and slope failure.

The presence of moderately inclined slopes with thick overburden, a jointed rock mass with relief joints, and small gullies or rills contributed to the conditions that facilitated slope failures under saturation. Heavy and continuous rainfall caused water to seep through open cracks and joints, as well as along the rock-overburden interface. This resulted in the saturation of the overburden mass and lubrication at the contact zone between the rock and overburden. When the driving forces exceeded the resisting forces, the oversaturated material failed along the contact plane, exposing fresh rock. The displaced material, combined with a large volume of water, transformed into a debris flow that traveled downslope for approximately 600 meters, devastating the entire Taliye village in its path. In this tragic event, the entire village of Taliye (Madhliwadi) was engulfed by a massive layer of debris, resulting in the destruction of approximately 42 houses. Reports indicate that a total of 84 people lost their lives. Additionally, the incident led to the loss of 40 cattle. Furthermore, vast agricultural lands suffered significant damage due to the scouring action of water and the deposition of displaced debris.
